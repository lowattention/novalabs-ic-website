About the IC

Where makers become entrepreneurs

The Innovation Center is a dedicated section of Nova Labs built for members who want to take their hobby to the next level. Whether you're testing a product idea or running a growing small business, the IC has a space for you.

We support local makers at every stage — from early experiments to established brands. You'll find a community of people who've been exactly where you are, along with the resources to keep moving forward.

What's here

Everything you need to grow

The IC brings together space, equipment, expertise, and community — all under one roof at Nova Labs.

Dedicated workspace

IC members get access to a dedicated section of Nova Labs set up specifically for product development and small business work.

Full equipment access

Laser cutters, 3D printers, CNC routers, woodworking tools, metalworking equipment — all available to IC members as part of Nova Labs.

Entrepreneur community

Connect with fellow IC members who are building businesses just like yours. Share knowledge, solve problems together, and cheer each other on.

Business resources

Workshops, mentorship sessions, and networking events designed to help you handle the business side — pricing, branding, selling, and more.

Marketplace visibility

Get your business featured in IC showcases, events, and online — putting your work in front of people who love to support local makers.

Classes & workshops

Learn new skills or sharpen existing ones with hands-on classes taught by experienced members and local experts.

In the making

Members building something new

Every successful IC business started with an idea and a workbench. Here are a few members in the early stages of building theirs.

Smart home

Sarah Chen

Member since 2023

Sarah is developing a line of low-cost smart home sensor kits aimed at renters who can't do permanent installations. She prototypes at Nova Labs every weekend, testing hardware iterations in the electronics lab.

"Being around other IC members keeps me going. When I get stuck, there's always someone who's been stuck in the exact same spot."

Toolmaking

Marcus Rivera

Member since 2022

Marcus is turning his obsession with hand-tool woodworking into a small-batch tool business — building marking gauges and chisels that compete with the expensive imports. He's using the IC's metalworking and finishing areas to dial in his process.

"I couldn't afford the equipment on my own. The IC made it possible to actually test my ideas without betting everything."

Laser craft

Fiona Okafor

Member since 2024

Fiona creates laser-cut educational puzzles and games for kids, aimed at making science and math more tactile. She's working on her first production run and figuring out packaging, pricing, and her first wholesale pitch.

"I knew how to make things, but the IC has been teaching me how to think about making things as a business."

3D printing

Ray Osei

Member since 2023

Ray is developing a line of custom 3D-printed ergonomic desk accessories. He's using the IC's printing lab to iterate on materials and finishes before committing to a small production run.

"The IC gave me access to equipment I'd never be able to afford on my own. That changes everything."

Leather goods

Nadia Flores

Member since 2022

Nadia hand-stitches minimalist leather wallets and bags using traditional saddle-stitch techniques. She's refining her patterns and building a small online shop while working through the IC's maker support program.

"I've learned more about running a business in six months here than in years of trying on my own."

Wearables

Tom Keller

Member since 2024

Tom is prototyping open-source wearable sensor patches for athletes and coaches who want data without the subscription fees. Early testers are already giving him feedback on comfort and accuracy.

"Having a community of engineers and makers to bounce ideas off makes the hard parts so much less lonely."
